Montessori Philosophy & Language Immersion

At Beibei Amigos Language Preschool, our program is grounded in the Montessori philosophy, which honors each child’s natural curiosity, independence, and love of learning. Children learn best through hands-on exploration, meaningful work, and guided discovery, and our classrooms are carefully prepared to support this process every day.

In every classroom, children engage in authentic Montessori works that develop concentration, critical thinking, fine motor skills, and a deep sense of confidence. Our teachers observe each child closely and provide individualized lessons based on readiness, allowing children to progress at their own pace while building a strong academic and emotional foundation.

Language immersion is fully integrated into our Montessori environment. Each classroom includes dedicated Mandarin and Spanish immersion teachers who guide children through daily routines, lessons, and social interactions entirely in the target language. Rather than teaching languages through memorization or drills, our teachers use natural conversation, songs, stories, and Montessori works to help children acquire language the same way they learned their first language—through meaningful, everyday experiences.

By combining Montessori education with Spanish and Mandarin immersion in every classroom, Beibei Amigos gives children the tools to think independently, communicate confidently, and engage with the world in multiple languages. This integrated approach prepares children not only for academic success, but also to become compassionate, capable, and globally minded learners.

The Five Areas of the Montessori Curriculum

Our Montessori Primary and Toddler classrooms include carefully curated materials across the five core Montessori areas:

Area One

Practical Life

Children learn everyday skills that build independence—pouring, scooping, buttoning, sweeping, food preparation, and caring for the environment. These activities strengthen fine motor skills, focus, coordination, confidence, and responsibility.

Area Two

Sensorial

Sensorial materials help children refine their senses and make sense of the world. Through hands-on exploration of color tablets, sound cylinders, geometric solids, and more, children develop critical thinking and early math and science foundations.

Area Three

Mathematics

Using concrete, hands-on materials such as number rods, golden beads, spindle boxes, and bead chains, children gain a deep understanding of numerals, place value, quantity, addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Area Four

Language

Through rich exposure to English, Spanish, and Mandarin, children acquire vocabulary, phonetic awareness, reading readiness, writing, and expressive communication. Each language is taught naturally by a native-speaking teacher.

Area Five

Cultural Studies

Children explore the world through maps, landforms, botany, zoology, cultural celebrations, and science activities. These lessons spark curiosity about the world and help children appreciate diversity.

Montessori “Works” That Inspire Growth

Purposeful Activities

In Montessori education, lessons are called works—purposeful activities designed to promote mastery through repetition, exploration, and self-correction. A child may choose a work like the pink tower, tracing sandpaper letters, matching sound cylinders, spooning beans, or labeling the parts of a plant.

Each work builds sequential skills and invites the child to concentrate deeply, think independently, and feel proud of their achievement.

Circle Time & The Work Cycle

Every day, children participate in circle time, where they greet one another, sing songs in English, Spanish, and Mandarin, discuss the calendar, practice social skills, and prepare for the work cycle.

After circle, the uninterrupted work period begins—one of the most important parts of the Montessori day. This is when children truly flourish, choosing materials that interest them and receiving individualized lessons from their teachers.
